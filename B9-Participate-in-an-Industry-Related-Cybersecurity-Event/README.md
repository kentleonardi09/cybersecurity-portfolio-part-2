## Event: CyberWest Hub Cybersecurity Event (CyberCX Talk)

### Description

I participated in a cybersecurity industry event hosted by CyberWest Hub, where Liam from the penetration testing team at CyberCX delivered a talk on career preparation in cybersecurity.

The session focused on what recruiters look for in candidates, how to build a strong CV, and how students can better prepare themselves for different cybersecurity roles. This event provided practical, real-world insights beyond academic learning.

### Explanation

From this event, I learned that recruiters in cybersecurity prioritise practical experience over academic performance alone. Hands-on activities such as labs, personal projects, and CTF challenges are highly valued, as they demonstrate real skills rather than theoretical knowledge. Recruiters also expect familiarity with industry-standard tools like Burp Suite, Nmap, and Wireshark, along with the ability to clearly communicate technical concepts. Since many candidates have similar academic backgrounds, what distinguishes applicants is initiative and demonstrated experience.

A key takeaway was the importance of building a strong project portfolio. A well-structured GitHub repository significantly improves employability by showcasing problem-solving skills, understanding of real-world vulnerabilities, and clear documentation. Examples of valuable projects include developing small security tools, writing vulnerability reports, and practising penetration testing in lab environments.

The talk also highlighted how to build an effective CV. A strong CV should be clear, concise, and tailored to the specific cybersecurity role being applied for. It should focus on technical skills, tools, certifications, and project experience rather than just education. Including links to GitHub or a portfolio is essential. Common mistakes to avoid include long paragraphs, irrelevant information, and generic descriptions.

Additionally, I gained a clearer understanding of different cybersecurity roles. Penetration testing focuses on offensive security and exploitation, SOC analysts focus on monitoring and incident response, and security engineers focus on designing and implementing secure systems. This emphasised the importance of choosing a specific career path and developing relevant skills accordingly.

Finally, the talk reinforced the importance of soft skills. Communication, teamwork, willingness to learn, and professionalism are all highly valued by recruiters. Being able to clearly explain your thought process is just as important as solving technical problems.

---

### Reflection

This event gave me a clearer understanding of how to prepare for a career in cybersecurity. I realised that success in this field is not just about academic performance, but about demonstrating practical skills, building a strong portfolio, and continuously improving.

As a result, I am motivated to improve my GitHub portfolio by adding more meaningful projects, engage in more hands-on cybersecurity practice, and tailor my CV towards specific roles within the industry.

Overall, this was a valuable experience that provided direct insight into industry expectations and helped bridge the gap between university learning and real-world cybersecurity careers.
