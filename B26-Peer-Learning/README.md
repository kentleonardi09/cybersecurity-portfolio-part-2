## 1. Peer Discussion – Honeypot & AppIDS

### Description
This activity involved a collaborative discussion with another student, Dana, where we helped each other understand key cybersecurity concepts. The interaction focused on two topics: honeypots and Application Intrusion Detection Systems (AppIDS).

During the discussion, Dana explained the concept of a honeypot to me, describing it as a decoy system designed to attract attackers so their actions can be monitored and analysed. This helped me understand how honeypots are used to study attack behaviour and improve security.

In return, I explained the concept of AppIDS to Dana. I described how it monitors activity within an application to detect suspicious behaviour such as SQL injection or unauthorised access attempts. I used simple and clear explanations to ensure the concept was easy to understand.

This interaction demonstrates effective communication, collaboration, and the ability to simplify complex cybersecurity concepts. It also highlights the importance of peer learning in strengthening understanding and reinforcing knowledge.
