## 1. UWA Computer Club – “Into the Cuckoo’s Egg”

### Description
For this activity, I attended a cybersecurity tech talk titled *“Into the Cuckoo’s Egg”* hosted by the UWA University Computer Club on 24 April and delivered by Mark Tearle. The session was based on a real-world scenario where a small $0.75 accounting anomaly led to the discovery of a cyber intrusion.

The talk explained how attackers gained unauthorised access through remote connections, escalated privileges to higher-level accounts, and explored systems to locate sensitive data. It also highlighted how system logs and monitoring were used to trace the attacker’s movements and identify suspicious activity.

Overall, this activity provided valuable insight into real-world cybersecurity practices and emphasised the importance of attention to detail, logging, and continuous monitoring in detecting and responding to security breaches.
