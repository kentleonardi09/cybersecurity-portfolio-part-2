# B21 - Cybersecurity Learning Activity

This section demonstrates my ability to design and implement an interactive cybersecurity learning activity to educate users and assess their awareness of online safety practices.

---

### Description
I created a cybersecurity learning activity using Google Forms to assess and improve participants' awareness of online security. The activity was designed to be simple, interactive, and suitable for general users with varying levels of cybersecurity knowledge.

The form was divided into four main sections: self-assessment, knowledge check, scenario-based questions, and learning reflection. The self-assessment section allowed participants to evaluate their own awareness and habits. The knowledge check tested understanding of key concepts such as strong passwords, phishing, and two-factor authentication (2FA).

Scenario-based questions were included to assess how participants would respond to real-life cybersecurity situations, such as suspicious messages and public Wi-Fi usage. Finally, the reflection section encouraged participants to think about what they learned and how they could improve their online behaviour.

---

### Implementation
The activity was distributed using Google Forms and completed by 7 participants. Responses were automatically collected and analysed using the platform’s summary tools, including charts and written answers.

---

### Findings
The results showed that most participants rated their cybersecurity awareness as moderate, indicating a basic understanding but room for improvement. Many participants reported that they only sometimes consider online security, suggesting that safe practices are not consistently applied.

Despite this, participants demonstrated strong knowledge of key concepts, with most correctly identifying phishing, strong passwords, and safe actions when handling suspicious emails. Scenario-based responses showed that participants generally understood how to avoid risks, such as ignoring suspicious links and avoiding sensitive activities on public Wi-Fi, although the level of detail varied.

In the reflection section, participants reported learning to be more cautious online, particularly when using public networks and interacting with unknown links. Overall, most participants reported increased confidence in their cybersecurity knowledge after completing the activity.

---

### Evaluation
The activity was effective in reinforcing basic cybersecurity concepts and increasing awareness. However, the results suggest a gap between knowledge and consistent behaviour, highlighting the need for continued education and practice.
