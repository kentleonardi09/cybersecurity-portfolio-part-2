## 1. PHIL1001 – Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) and AI Ethics

### Description
This lecture explored the ethical implications of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI), focusing on issues such as bias, accountability, transparency, and the societal impact of highly autonomous systems.

### Key Learnings
- AGI systems can make complex decisions similar to humans
- Ethical risks include bias, lack of accountability, and misuse
- Importance of fairness, transparency, and explainability in AI

### Why It Matters (Cyber Ethics/Law)
AGI introduces challenges in responsibility, regulation, and data privacy. Legal systems must adapt to ensure ethical AI deployment and prevent harm caused by autonomous decision-making.

### Personal Reflection
This lecture helped me understand that cybersecurity is not just technical but also ethical. Protecting systems also means ensuring they are used responsibly and fairly.

## 2. CITS2006 Guest Lecture – Fraud Analyst (Banking Cybersecurity)

### Description
This guest lecture provided valuable insights into cybersecurity roles within the banking industry, focusing on fraud detection, threat monitoring, risk management, and protecting sensitive financial data.

### Key Learnings
- Fraud detection uses behavioral analysis and anomaly detection
- Cybersecurity teams monitor suspicious transactions in real-time
- Risk management is critical in financial systems

### Why It Matters (Cyber Ethics/Law)
Handling financial data requires strict compliance with privacy laws. Ethical concerns arise when monitoring systems impact user privacy or incorrectly flag legitimate activity.

### Personal Reflection
This session gave me a clearer understanding of real-world cybersecurity careers. It showed how ethical responsibility plays a major role in protecting users and maintaining trust in financial systems.
