## Overview
This section demonstrates my participation in two cybersecurity-related talks and my understanding of real-world cybersecurity concepts gained through these events.

## 1. Cybersecurity for Small Businesses (OSINT Talk)

### Description
For this activity, I attended a cybersecurity talk focused on open-source intelligence (OSINT) and its impact on individuals and small businesses. The session explained how attackers can gather information using publicly available sources such as social media, websites, and search engines.

The speaker highlighted how attackers often rely on human behaviour rather than technical vulnerabilities, using collected information to carry out targeted attacks like phishing and impersonation. The talk emphasised that freelancers and small businesses are particularly vulnerable due to their online presence and limited security measures.

---

### Explanation
I looked at how attackers use OSINT (open-source intelligence) to gather information about individuals and businesses using only publicly available data. It was explained that attackers can collect data from sources such as social media, company websites, public records, and search engines to build detailed profiles. This information can then be used to perform targeted attacks such as phishing or social engineering, especially against small businesses and freelancers.

---

### What I’ve Learned
I found out that a large amount of personal and professional information about me can be accessed online without hacking, simply through public sources. This showed me that cybersecurity depends heavily on human behaviour. I learned that reducing my digital footprint and being cautious about what I share online can significantly improve my personal security.

## 2. Unicorn Dispatch LIVE: Cybersecurity Education Meets Real-World Emulation

### Description
For this activity, I attended a cybersecurity seminar focused on modern approaches to cybersecurity education. The session discussed how training is shifting from theory-based learning to practical, hands-on experiences using simulation and emulation platforms.

The speakers explained how combining offensive and defensive cybersecurity strategies helps learners better understand real-world threats. This approach allows participants to practice detecting and responding to cyber attacks in realistic environments.

---

### Explanation
I looked at how cybersecurity education is evolving to better prepare people for real-world cyber threats. The event focused on simulation and emulation platforms that allow learners to practice defending against cyber attacks in controlled environments. It introduced the idea of combining both attacking and defending strategies, helping learners understand how cyber threats work and how organisations respond to them.

---

### What I’ve Learned
I found out that hands-on experience is essential in cybersecurity, not just theoretical knowledge. Practicing in simulated environments helps develop real skills in detecting and responding to cyber threats. This approach better prepares individuals for real-world cybersecurity challenges and highlights the importance of continuous learning and practice.
